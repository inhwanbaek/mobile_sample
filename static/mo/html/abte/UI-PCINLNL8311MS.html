Audit Committee

Evaluates and improves the overall adequacy of internal controls and business performance. Audit Committee is assuring independence from the BOD and the management in its auditing activities.

Main Duties
  • Planning, execution, and evaluation of internal audits including financial audit, operational audit, compliance audit, management audit, and IT audit.
  • Evaluation of overall internal control systems, and proposal of improvements.
  • Agreement on appointment of head of the audit supporting team
  • Approval of independent auditor appointment and evaluation of its auditing activities.

Management Committee

Makes timely decisions on the following issues as delegated by the BOD, and reports to the BOD on the result of execution.

Main Duties
  • Establishment of long and short-term management policy and management plan.
  • Management-related matters such as launching a new business.
  • Financial-related matters such as disposal and transfer of major assets
  • Executes the evaluation of directors and executive officers and establishes compensation scheme for executive officers

Executive Recommendation Committee

In accordance with Governance Act for Financial Institution which is effective on and from August 1, 2016, Kyobo Life established Executive Recommendation Committee in order to ensure transparency in appointing outside directors, CEO, and audit committee members. Candidates for outside directors, CEO, and audit committee members shall be recommended by Executive Recommendation Committee and the final appointment shall be made in the General Shareholders' meeting or Board of Directors meeting.

Main Duties
  • Recommendation of candidates for executives which exclusively include outside directors, CEO and audit committee members
  • Management of the outside director candidate pool
  • Management of the CEO candidate pool

Risk Management Committee

Company-wide risk management is one of the core competencies of the insurance business. Kyobo Life established the Risk Management Committee under the BOD to systemically operate risk management organizations and make risk management more sophisticated. The Risk Management Committee regularly analyzes risk, and reports to the BOD on risk management plan, monitoring results, etc.

Main Duties
  • Establishment of basic direction and major policies related to the risk management.
  • Approval of the company's major management strategies based on their risks.
  • Set up of total risk, and risk limits for divisions, new products, and new investments.
  • Determines appropriate investment and loss limits.
  • Approval of strategies to secure and maintain capital adequacy based on risk based capital system

Remuneration Committee

In principle, a member of the Risk Management Committee is also appointed as a member of the Remuneration Committee. This composition allows the Remuneration Committee to design and operate the company's remuneration scheme in line with risk management, independently from top management. Specifically, in order to make sure the remuneration scheme will reflect financially sound practices, the Remuneration Committee designs the remuneration scheme so that top management, and those engaged in certain tasks, will not take an excessive level of risk to receive a higher remuneration.

Sustainable ESG Committee

Deliberates and decides on important environmental, social, and governance matters or matters delegated by the BOD as the final decision-making body of ESG management.

Main Duties
  • Approval of strategies for sustainable management (ESG management) and relevant major policies, etc.
